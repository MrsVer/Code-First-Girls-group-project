Food

Nigerian cuisine consists of dishes or food items from the hundreds of ethnic groups that comprise Nigeria. Like other West African cuisines, it uses spices and herbs with palm or groundnut oil to create deeply flavored sauces and soups. Nigerian food consists of so many flavours and ingredients, but mainly revolves around the staples of rice, beans, greens, and root vegetables such as yam and cassava. With such wide variety, there is bound to be something for everyone. There are hundreds of street food vendors around Lagos serving authentic Nigerian foods. Many restaurants and bars in both the mainland and the islands also provide the chance to sample some of Nigerias delicious food. Some must-try dishes are Jollof Rice and Beef Suya.

Tourist Spots

There are over a dozen beaches in Lagos, making for a wide array to choose from; These are great for picnics and barbecues. The Lekki Conservation Centre by the Lekki Conservation Foundation, is a 78-hectare Natural Resource Conservation. It protects the wetlands of the Lekki peninsula which consists of swamp and savannah habitats, hosting many plants and animals like monkeys, snakes, crocodiles and various birds. It is also home to Africa's largest canopy walkway!

Culture

Like Nigeria itself, which is home to 180 million people, 300 tribes and 500 languages, Lagos is a melting pot of cultural traditions. The city is at the centre of the most important film industry in Africa, Nollywood, which produces around 1,500 films each year. Music is a huge part of Nigerian culture, and there is no better place to experience it than Lagos as it is one of the most important homes of the Afrobeats music movement. Nigeria's culture and history is reflected in its art, which can be found in one of the many galleries on display in Lagos.
