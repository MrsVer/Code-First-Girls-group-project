Food

Traditional Vietnamese cooking is greatly admired for its fresh ingredients, minimal use of dairy and oil, complementary textures, and reliance on herbs and vegetables. With the balance between fresh herbs and meats and a selective use of spices to reach a fine taste, Vietnamese food is considered one of the healthiest cuisines worldwide. French cuisine has also had a major influence due to the French colonization of Vietnam. Some must-try dishes are Bun Cha and Cao Lau.

Tourist Spots

Hội An is a city on Vietnam’s central coast known for its well-preserved Ancient Town (which is also a UNESCO World Cultural Heritage site!), cut through with canals. The former port city’s melting-pot history is reflected in its architecture, a mix of eras and styles from wooden Chinese shophouses and temples to colorful French colonial buildings, ornate Vietnamese tube houses and the iconic Japanese Covered Bridge with its pagoda..

Culture

The culture of Hoi An is characterised by a harmonious blend of Vietnamese, Chinese and Japanese influences. Hoi An is recognized as an exceptionally well-preserved example of a Southeast Asian trading port dating from the 15th to the 19th century, its buildings and street plan reflecting a blend of indigenous and foreign influences. After many centuries, Hoi An is still respectful of its traditions, folk festivals, beliefs and of its sophisticated culinary art.
